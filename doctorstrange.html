Doctor Strange

Stephen Strange, also known as Doctor Strange, is a brilliant surgeon who becomes the Sorcerer Supreme after gaining mastery over the mystic arts and protecting the world from supernatural threats.

Doctor Strange is a superhero created by Stan Lee and Steve Ditko who appears in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. The character first appeared in Strange Tales #110 (December 1963), and was later featured in The Avengers #85 (October 1963). Doctor Strange is a member of the superhero team the Avengers and has been portrayed as one of Marvel's most popular characters.

Doctor Strange's character has undergone several changes over the years. In his early appearances, he was depicted as a more traditional superhero with a strong moral code. Over time, his character evolved to include more complex themes such as his relationship with his teammates and his struggles with power and responsibility.

Doctor Strange has been adapted into various forms of media including films, television series, animated shows, and video games. His portrayal in these adaptations has often emphasized his strength, leadership qualities, and his role as a protector of both Asgard and Earth.
